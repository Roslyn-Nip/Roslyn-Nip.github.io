Remind is a reminder based app for planning that is designed for users that prefer reminder based task lists rather than notes based task planning apps.

Overview

Problem statement

How might we streamline the planning process for users that primarily use reminders for planning?

Our Solution

Based on user interviews and market research, I created a simple reminder based planning application to allow for scheduled reminder in a relaxed and organized format.

User Interviews

In this project I wanted to create a product based on user interviews so I created some questions and interviewed 3 participants. I collected and summarised my notes and created a miro board to capture what I got from my participants. I summarised my results into 6 categories: Emotions when Planning, Most Helpful Features, Planning Habits, Current Planning Application features, Pain Points, and Values. In general I found that simplicity and convenience was a major aspect in planning as well as the degree of motivation and control over one’s own planning. Since I didn’t want to reinvent the wheel and compete with lots of notes based task planning apps, I decided to centre my app around reminder planning with customizable notifications.

Takeaways

Simplicity is key

As this is one self directed project, I learned a lot about incorporating research into design and executing it as an app prototype. As a common barrier was complicated interfaces, I tried to prioritise flows that would be mostly utilitarian with a simple aesthetic.
